At sunrise the sand at Sossusvlei looks like something that has been on fire for five million years, because in a slow, patient way it has. Iron oxide coats every grain; each dawn re-lights it.

Some of these dunes are over 300 meters tall. Wind reshapes their crests daily, but the great mother-dunes have held their positions for millennia.
