Skogafoss falls in a sheet so wide and so consistent that on sunny days a rainbow — sometimes a double — permanently hangs at its base. Local legend says a Viking settler named Þrasi Þórólfsson buried a chest of gold in the cave behind the falls. Whether or not there was ever gold, the mist itself is enough.

Climb the 527 stairs on the east side for a view along the river as it braids toward the black-sand coast.
