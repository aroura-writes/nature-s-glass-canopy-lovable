The redwood canopy behaves like a second sky. On damp mornings the fog condenses on needles two hundred feet above the forest floor and drips down for hours after the fog itself has burned off — a private rainfall that the trees have engineered for themselves.

## Where to see it

- **Redwood National Park**, California
- **Muir Woods**, just north of San Francisco
- **Prairie Creek Redwoods State Park** at dawn

> Stand still long enough and you will hear the trees drink.
