The water above a healthy reef is a lens: sunlight enters the surface, scatters through living coral, and returns to the eye as a hundred blues at once. This is the shallow lagoon at the edge of a South Pacific atoll, where the drop-off falls into deep sapphire within a single boat-length.

## Field notes

- Best light: 10 AM – 2 PM, sun overhead
- Look for parrotfish grazing on coral rubble — you can hear them crunch
- Never touch the coral. Even a fingerprint of sunscreen can bleach it.
